AI ON THE PITCH: OPTIMIZING PLAYER SELECTION IN SOCCER

Introduction/Background

Machine learning can be applied to sports analytics to get insights to improve a team's performance. Since traditional approaches rely on subjective judgements, optimizing team performance through a data-driven approach becomes crucial.

Literature Review

Recent studies have used machine learning to address team formation and player selection. Unsupervised methods like GMMs and K-means cluster teams based on goals, and supervised learning helps identify optimal team compositions.

Problem Definition

Problem

Soccer teams play using various styles; however, optimizing these strategies and choosing the best players for a particular opponent can be a difficult task. Rather than using a data-driven approach to cluster teams, coaches oftern rely on subjective judgements that an lead to poor team performance in games.

Motivation

Automating the analysis of teams to explore the best playing styles can allow teams to make better and strategic decisions. Using unsupervised learning to cluster teams based on tactics and supervised learning to predict match outcomes, this project will provide concrete insights to help teams to best select their players and strategy.

Methods

Data Preprocessing Methods Identified

  1. Exploratory Data Analysis to find important features
  2. Feature Engineering - to make relevant combinations of features
  3. Principal Component Analysis for Dimensionality Reduction

Unsupervised Learning

    Using K-means, we will cluster teams based on their playing styles. The optimal number of clusters will be determined using the elbow method with t-SNE being used for cluster visualization. To capture the finer team details, we will use GMMs for flexible clustering and Hierarchical Clustering for multi-level similarity analysis.
  1. Method 1: K-Means + GMM
  2. Method 2: Hierarchial Clustering

Supervised Learning

    We hope to create a classification system based on the StatsBomb dataset. Using ensemble methods including XGBoost, Random Forest, and Support Vector Machines, we hope to categorize players by role. By analyzing features such as physical attributes, technical skills, tactical awareness, and performance statistics, we aim to enhance tactical analysis and understand player dynamics within teams.
  1. Method 1: SVM [3].
  2. Method 2: Random Forest
  3. Method 3: Gradient Boost

    4. Integrating Supervised and Unsupervised Learning

    This integrated approach combines clustering techniques to identify team playing styles with supervised learning methods for player classification and performance evaluation. By merging these analyses, we create a comprehensive system that can assess how well individual players fit into specific team compositions and predict potential match outcomes when considering lineup changes or transfers.

Project Goals

The project aims to group soccer teams by their playing styles to provide insights on improving strategies. Moreover, we hope to optimize player selection based on the opposition and predict outcomes to reduce the subjective decision making and provide new strategies backed by data.

Expected Results

We hope to identify clusters of teams with similar playing styles offering insights to coaches to devise new strategies. Our system will also player selection based off their opposition. We also hope to predict match outcomes with a 75% accuracy. Additionally, clustering should reveal trends that lead to success providing insights into how playing styles evolve.
