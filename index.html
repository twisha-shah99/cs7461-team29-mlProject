CS7461 Team 29 - ML Project Proposal

Optimizing Player Selection for Soccer Using Machine Learning

Problem Overview

Problem

Football teams play using various styles; however, optimizing these strategies and choosing the best players for a particular opponent can be a difficult task. Coaches often rely on subjective judgments that can lead to suboptimal decisions, affecting team performance.

Motivation

Automating the analysis of teams to explore the best playing styles enables better, data-driven strategic decisions. This project will use unsupervised learning to cluster teams based on tactics and supervised learning to predict match outcomes, providing concrete insights to improve player selection and team strategies, enhancing their performance in games.

Methods

Data Preprocessing

  • Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) to identify key features.
  • Feature Engineering for relevant combinations.
  • Principal Component Analysis (PCA) for Dimensionality Reduction.

Unsupervised Learning

Team Cluster Analysis

  • K-Means + GMM: Clustering teams by playing styles (goals scored, possession, etc.).
  • Hierarchical Clustering: For deeper team similarity analysis.

Supervised Learning

Player Classification

  • Support Vector Machines (SVM)
  • Random Forest
  • Gradient Boosting

Potential Results and Discussion

We anticipate the development of a system that effectively clusters teams by playing style, predicts match outcomes, and optimizes player selection. Our goal is to provide actionable insights to coaches, enabling them to make better strategic decisions based on data rather than intuition.

Key Metrics

  • F1 Score: Targeting 0.8 or higher for player classification.
  • RMSE: Aiming for 0.5 or lower in regression tasks.
  • Adjusted Rand Index (ARI): Aim for 0.6+ in clustering accuracy.
  • Accuracy: Predict match outcomes with 75% or higher accuracy.
